HEBP1, Heme-binding protein 1

May bind free porphyrinogens that may be present in the cell and thus facilitate removal of these potentially toxic compound. Binds with a high affinity to one molecule of heme or porphyrins. It binds metalloporphyrins, free porphyrins and N-methylprotoporphyrin with similar affinities.


UniProtKB ID: Q9NQF3

Sequence Selection in Uniprot, Identity filtering in PDB

I looked up proteins in Uniprot, filtering by enzyme, length of 150-250 aa, not in PDB and protein existence is uncertain. This gave me a total of 31 hits in Uniprot, of which I chose SERHL_HUMAN, Serine hydrolase-like protein

I then ran it in PDB to find structures with similar sequences, resulting in 18 structures with identity no greater than 41% but at least 30%. All of these structures were either hydrolases or oxidoreductases found in bacteria.

3D Structure Prediction in SwissModel and Phyre2

I used SwissModel and Phyre2 to predict protein structure. SwissModel Model 1 was chosen because it had 40% sequence identity compared to 24% in Model 2.



Structure Validation in Procheck

I used Procheck to verify the predicted structure. Procheck checks the stereochemical quality of a protein structure by analyzing residue-by-residue geometry and overall structure geometry.

Results:

2/8 checks passed, including ramachandran, with warnings, and chi1-chi2 plots. There were outliers in the ramachandran plots with unfavorable dihedral angles. Whereas, the chi1-chi2 plots had no oultiers. The chi1 chi2 side chain torsion angle combinations for all residue types whose side chains are sufficiently long to have these angles



Domain and Predicted Structure Mutation

According to Uniprot, the SERHL_HUMAN protein has a hydrolase domain in positions 33-145. Residues GPPV 32-35 were removed from the beginning of the protein domain to see if it wouls cause a change in structure because Proline is known for causing turns. The mutated protein has a beta sheet in place of a loop, confirming that the prolines were causing a turn and prohibiting a beta sheet or helix. The addition of a beta sheet in the hydrolaze domain could prohibit binding and in turn function.
